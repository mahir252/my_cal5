1.Motion (Goti) in Physics 1. Definition of Motion (Goti) In physics, motion is defined as: A body is said to be in motion if its position changes with time with respect to a chosen reference point or observer. Key points you must understand (no shortcuts): Motion is relative, not absolute. Without a reference point, motion has no meaning. Time must be involved. Example: A student sitting inside a moving bus is: At rest with respect to the bus In motion with respect to the road or trees outside So, motion depends on who is observing and from where.



2. Why Reference Point is Mandatory Physics does not accept vague statements like: ‚ÄúThe object is moving.‚Äù You must say: ‚ÄúThe object is moving with respect to something.‚Äù Example: A book on a table: At rest with respect to the table In motion with respect to the Sun (because Earth is moving) This is why we say: There is no absolute rest or absolute motion in classical mechanics.



3. Types of Motion (Prokar of Goti) In basic physics, motion is mainly classified into the following types: A. Translational Motion Definition: When all points of a body move the same distance in the same direction in the same time, the motion is called translational motion. The body moves as a whole. Subtypes of Translational Motion: 1. Rectilinear Motion Definition: Motion of a body along a straight line is called rectilinear motion. Examples: A car moving on a straight road A stone falling vertically downward A train moving on a straight track This is the simplest form of motion and is heavily used in kinematics.
2. Curvilinear Motion Definition: Motion of a body along a curved path is called curvilinear motion. Examples: A thrown ball following a curved path A car taking a bend on a curved road Water flowing through a curved pipe ‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Curvilinear motion is still translational motion Only the path is curved


B. Rotational Motion Definition: When a body rotates about a fixed axis, it is said to be in rotational motion. Here: The axis may be inside or outside the body Different points move in circular paths Examples: A ceiling fan rotating about its central axis Earth rotating about its own axis A wheel rotating around its axle üî¥ Key difference from translational motion: In rotational motion, different points have different linear speeds
C. Circular Motion Definition: Motion of a body along the circumference of a circle is called circular motion. Examples: A stone tied to a string and whirled in a circle The second hand of a clock A satellite revolving around Earth ‚ö†Ô∏è Important Concept: Circular motion is a special case of curvilinear motion Even if speed is constant, velocity is changing (direction changes)
D. Oscillatory Motion Definition: A motion in which a body moves to and fro about a fixed mean position repeatedly is called oscillatory motion. Key properties: There is a mean (equilibrium) position Motion is periodic Examples: Simple pendulum Swing in a playground Mass attached to a spring
E. Periodic Motion Definition: A motion that repeats itself after equal intervals of time is called periodic motion. Examples: Earth‚Äôs revolution around the Sun Rotation of Earth about its axis Oscillation of a pendulum ‚ö†Ô∏è Relation: All oscillatory motions are periodic But not all periodic motions are oscillatory
F. Random Motion Definition: Motion in which a body moves in an irregular and unpredictable path is called random motion. Examples: Motion of gas molecules Movement of dust particles in air Brownian motion This motion has: No fixed path No fixed direction 4. Rest and Motion Are Relative This is an exam-favorite concept. Statement: Rest and motion are relative terms and depend on the observer. Example: A passenger sleeping in a bus: At rest relative to the bus In motion relative to the road There is no universal frame where everything is at rest. 5.
