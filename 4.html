FORCE




Force (Bol) in Physics 1. What is Force? (Definition) In physics, force is defined as: A force is a push or a pull which can change the shape, size, speed, direction, or state of rest of a body. In simple words: Force is anything that can: Start motion Stop motion Change speed Change direction Deform an object ⚠️ Important: Force is a vector quantity It has magnitude and direction SI unit of force is newton (N)




2. Effects of Force A force can: Change the state of rest or motion Change speed (increase or decrease) Change direction of motion Change shape or size of an object Example: Kicking a football → changes speed Catching a moving ball → stops motion Pressing clay → changes shape




3. Types of Force Forces are mainly classified into two broad types:




A. Contact Forces Definition: Forces that act only when two bodies are in physical contact are called contact forces.




1. Muscular Force Definition: The force exerted by muscles of living beings is called muscular force. Examples: Lifting a bag Pushing a table Pulling a cart ⚠️ Acts only when muscles are used.




2. Frictional Force Definition: The force that opposes the relative motion between two surfaces in contact is called friction. Examples: Walking without slipping Brakes stopping a car Rubbing hands to produce heat ⚠️ Direction: Always opposite to motion




B. Non-Contact Forces Definition: Forces that act without physical contact between objects are called non-contact forces.




1. Gravitational Force Definition: The force by which the Earth attracts objects towards its center is called gravitational force. Examples: A stone falling to the ground Rain falling from clouds Objects having weight ⚠️ Always attractive in nature.




2. Magnetic Force Definition: The force exerted by a magnet on magnetic materials or another magnet is called magnetic force. Examples: Magnet attracting iron nails Repulsion between like poles of magnets Compass needle aligning north-south ⚠️ Can be attractive or repulsive.




3. Electrostatic Force Definition: The force between electrically charged bodies is called electrostatic force. Examples: Balloon sticking to a wall after rubbing Paper bits attracted by a charged comb Repulsion between like charges ⚠️ Acts even in vacuum.




4. Applied Force Definition: A force that is applied to an object by a person or another object is called applied force. Examples: Pushing a door Pulling a rope Kicking a football




5. Normal Force Definition: The force exerted by a surface perpendicular to the object resting on it is called normal force. Examples: A book resting on a table A person standing on the ground ⚠️ Always acts perpendicular to the surface.




6. Tension Force Definition: The force transmitted through a string, rope, or cable when it is pulled tight is called tension. Examples: Hanging a bucket from a rope Pulling a load with a rope




7. Air Resistance (Drag Force) Definition: The force that opposes the motion of an object moving through air is called air resistance. Examples: Falling feather reaching ground slowly Parachute slowing down a skydiver
